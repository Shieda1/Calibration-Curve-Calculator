What is the calibration curve?
The calibration curve is a general method in analytical chemistry that measures the concentration of a substance in an unknown sample by comparing the unknown to a set of samples of know concentration.

Why use calibration curve?
The calibration curve is used to determine the concentration of an unknown sample in order to measure the limit of detection and also the limit of quantitation.
